This poster argues that for a politician interested in retaining office protecting forested areas is efficient in most years, but giving targeted access to pivotal voters is efficient in election years. I test the theory that competitive elections are associated with higher rates of deforestation using remote sensed satellite data of forest cover and data on national elections.
